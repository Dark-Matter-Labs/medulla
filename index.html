info@medulla.city
Dresdener Str. 113, Berlin

Medulla connects work across governance, technology, culture, economy, and civic practice — building resilience under conditions of compounding crisis. It is a carefully held environment for people who want their workplace to be part of a wider field of attention, experimentation, and consequence.

Located in a Berlin courtyard on ground once marked by the division of East and West Berlin, medulla explores how resilience moves between bodies and scales: neighbourhood, city, planet. Deeply local in its setting and international in its relations, the space makes that polarity explicit.

Resilience

medulla is spread across two levels. The upper floor is oriented toward focused work: desks, quieter zones, and the ordinary discipline of getting things done.

The lower floor is more open and communal: a shared table, kitchen, workshop and gathering space, and room for small sessions, reviews, meals, and conversations.

There is also access to a courtyard and garden — a place to step outside the task at hand, reset attention, or care for the living world around us.

Space

We invite a small number of organisations and practitioners who want their work to be part of a larger shared environment. You might work in public administration, technology, governance, urban planning, finance, culture, or civic practice.

What matters less than sector is seriousness: a willingness to work on real questions, in a place where different kinds of work can touch.

Contributions currently range from 300 to 1,500 EUR per month. Each contribution supports more than occupancy — it sustains the shared environment and its curatorial direction.

Members

Reach out at hello@medulla.city if you have questions about membership, collaboration, or the project.

medulla is initiated by Politics for Tomorrow / nextlearning e.V. and Dark Matter Labs.

Contacts

On an ordinary day, medulla might hold several worlds at once — someone working on heat preparedness for a district administration, a civic technologist mapping public interfaces, a foundation team shaping new approaches to urban resilience.

Some days are quiet and concentrated. Others gather momentum through project reviews, workshops, shared inquiries, public conversations, or small experimental formats.

What matters is that the space supports simultaneously: deep individual work, coordinated exchange, and collaboration across fields.
