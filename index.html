Medulla is open to collaboration with people, organizations, and initiatives working toward collective resilience, civic imagination, and new models for shared prosperity. We welcome conversations with those interested in contributing to the space, joining the community, or developing work in dialogue with Medulla's wider mission.

Our public programming is designed to be shaped with others, bringing together lectures, salons, workshops, and gatherings that connect different fields and communities. If your work resonates with Medulla's purpose, we are open to exploring how it could become part of the life of the space.

contacts

Medulla is made up of multiple organizations and individuals working toward resilience in their own unique ways. Our work spans different fields, communities, and practices, but is connected by a shared commitment to building the capacities, relationships, and infrastructures needed to navigate crisis and create more liveable futures.

We are currently assembling the first cohort of Medulla members and are open to applications from organizations and individuals who want to join the space on a permanent basis. Medulla is intended to be more than a shared office: it is a community of practice where different forms of knowledge, action, and care can strengthen one another.

We believe change needs to happen in tangible places: in rooms, courtyards, buildings, and neighborhoods where people can gather, build trust, and work together. Situated in a quiet Kreuzberg courtyard, Dresdener 113B serves as both a living room and a working space for developing the relationships, ideas, and infrastructures needed for resilient collective futures.

The ground floor hosts Medulla's public programming, including salons, lectures, workshops, gatherings, and other formats designed to build resilience, prosperity, and new civic imagination. The floor above is dedicated to focused work, hosting member organizations and individuals whose work contributes to Medulla's shared mission in distinct but mutually reinforcing ways.

Medulla is a physical space for turning shared urgency into collective resilience by building new collaborations and infrastructures of prosperity.

Medulla takes its name from Medulla Oblongata, a part of the human brain and a critical command center for involuntary, life-sustaining functions such as breathing, heart rate, and blood pressure. Taken as inspiration, Medulla is a response to the growing and compounding crisis we all viscerally experience today.

It brings together organizations, practitioners, and communities working across different fields to respond to the technical, ecological, economic, and cultural crises shaping our shared future.
